Gagan's Healthy Cricket Routine

This routine is made for me, a 12-year-old boy who has diabetes and loves to play cricket

Morning Stretch

Start the day with gentle stretching for the arms, legs, back, and neck. Keep it slow and easy.

Time: 10 minutes

Healthy Breakfast

Eat a balanced breakfast with protein, fruit, and slow-energy carbs. This helps keep blood sugar steady.

Time: 15 minutes

Cricket Practice

Do light batting, bowling, and fielding drills. Keep practice fun, active, and not too exhausting.

Time: 35-45 minutes

Light Strength

Try simple bodyweight moves like squats, lunges, and gentle push-ups. Focus on control and good form.

Time: 15 minutes

Evening Walk

Go for a calm walk or play outside after school. This helps with fitness and keeps the body active.

Time: 20 minutes

Rest and Hydration

Drink water often, rest well, and follow the diabetes plan from the doctor or parent. Avoid overtraining.

Time: All day

Safety Tip: For a child with diabetes, exercise should be safe and supervised. Check with a doctor, keep water ready, and make sure Gagan eats healthy meals at the right times.
