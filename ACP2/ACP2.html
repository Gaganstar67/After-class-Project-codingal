Mahatma Gandhi

Biography of Mahatma Gandhi Ji

Early Life

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on 2nd October 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat. He is widely honored as Mahatma Gandhi and often addressed respectfully as Gandhi Ji.

His father, Karamchand Gandhi, served as the diwan of Porbandar. His mother, Putlibai, influenced him with a deep sense of truth and compassion. Gandhi Ji grew up in a household that valued religion, self-discipline, and service.

Education and Personal Life

Gandhi traveled to London in 1888 to study law. He returned to India in 1891 and later spent 21 years in South Africa. His experience there shaped his belief in nonviolence and civil rights.

In 1883, Gandhi married Kasturba Makhanji. The couple had four sons: Harilal, Manilal, Ramdas, and Devdas.

Key Principles

Gandhi Ji introduced several important principles:

Satyagraha - the force of truth
Ahimsa - nonviolence
Swaraj - self-rule and independence

Major Movements

He led many movements against British rule, including the Champaran Satyagraha, the Salt March in 1930, and the Quit India Movement in 1942.

The Salt March began on 12th March 1930, when Gandhi Ji walked 240 miles to protest the salt tax.

Philosophy and Legacy

Gandhi believed that society should be built on justice, equality, and compassion. He used fasting, peaceful protest, and self-reliance to change public opinion.

His influence was not limited to India; leaders around the world, such as Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela, were inspired by his teachings.

Later Years and Death

After India gained independence in 1947, Gandhi continued to work for harmony between different religious communities. On 30th January 1948, he was assassinated in New Delhi by Nathuram Godse.

Remembering Gandhi Ji

Mahatma Gandhi remains a symbol of peace and courage. His life reminds us that even ordinary people can create extraordinary change through love, truth, and nonviolence.

Note: Gandhi Ji is often called Bapu, meaning father of the nation, and his birthday is celebrated as Gandhi Jayanti every year.
